Release Notes

What's new in Not There Yet.

A record of new games, compatibility changes, and improvements in each version.

v1.3.1

Support for iOS 16 and iPadOS 16

Not There Yet now supports iOS 16 and iPadOS 16. This makes it compatible with a wider range of older iPhone and iPad models that families may already use as shared devices.

v1.3

Roadside Challenge

Roadside Challenge joins five other family road-trip games with a new turn-based way to play. Each player gets a prompt, and the whole Road Crew helps look. Take turns spotting the unexpected and race to the target score.

  • New turn-based Roadside Challenge game
  • 128 prompts in the included Classic Road Trip deck
  • Adjustable target score
  • Saved progress when an Adventure is interrupted
  • Final scoreboards and tie handling

v1.2

iPad and Adventure Log improvements

This update makes Not There Yet more comfortable on iPad and easier to follow throughout an Adventure. Completed games are now organized in the Adventure Log, Road Chain decisions are clearer, and game summaries are clearer.

  • Improved layouts for iPad
  • Renamed Trip History to Adventure Log
  • Grouped completed games by trip, crew, and day
  • Added clearer Road Chain elimination and end-game confirmations
  • Refined winner and trip-summary wording

v1.1

Roadside Oddities and clearer gameplay

This update introduced Roadside Oddities, expanded the spotting prompts, and made each game easier to follow based on early feedback.

v1.0

First App Store release

The first release introduced Road Crew setup, five road-trip games, Adventure flow, the original Trip History, player stats, and in-app Support and Privacy information.
